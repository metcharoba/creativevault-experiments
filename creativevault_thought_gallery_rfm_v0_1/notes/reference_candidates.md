# Reference Candidates — CreativeVault Thought Gallery RFM v0.1

## Existing Register Check

Recent builds in `70_design_experiments/` cluster around:

- Quiet archival/riso editorial.
- Loud primary-color pop/comic.
- Signal/transmission motifs and "future/ideas/fragments" vocabulary.
- Halftone, misregistration, cream paper, red/blue/yellow accents.
- Condensed-bold display or high-contrast serif/italic pairing.

This build should avoid those tics and test a more personal gallery/register: sparse, object-led, exhibition-like, and slightly private.

## Candidate A — The Museum of Jurassic Technology

- URL: https://www.mjt.org/
- Why: It behaves like a private cabinet of knowledge rather than a conventional museum site. The copy and pacing make knowledge feel strange, intimate, and curated.
- Borrow: ceremonial entry, sparse object-like page composition, sense of one person arranging mysteries.
- Do not borrow: faux-antique murk, obscure navigation that makes the homepage unusable, or direct cabinet-of-curiosity theming.

## Candidate B — Dia Art Foundation

- URL: https://www.diaart.org/
- Why: It treats a collection as a constellation of locations, sites, programs, and works. Strong fit for a homepage that gathers different kinds of personal output without making them look like SaaS cards.
- Borrow: spatial indexing, severe typography, large whitespace, title-first hierarchy, restrained image rhythm.
- Do not borrow: institutional donation/news clutter, generic museum navigation density, or overly cold minimalism.

## Candidate C — WebMuseum

- URL: https://www.ibiblio.org/wm/
- Why: An early virtual museum/collection model. It is useful as a structural reference for a personal archive that feels handmade, direct, and non-commercial.
- Borrow: humble directory logic, text-as-navigation, artwork/thought entries as a browsable index.
- Do not borrow: raw 1990s browser defaults, low readability, or nostalgic pastiche.

## Candidate Set 2 — New Direction

### Candidate 2A — The Serving Library

- URL: https://www.servinglibrary.org/
- Why: It is almost all index, titles, authors, and collection logic. The whole page behaves like a living bookshelf rather than a homepage template.
- Borrow: dense title river, publication/library atmosphere, visible taxonomy, the idea that the archive itself is the visual material.
- Do not borrow: text-only austerity, overwhelming endless list, broken/garbled text rendering, or academic coldness.

### Candidate 2B — Cabinet Magazine

- URL: https://www.cabinetmagazine.org/
- Why: It treats culture as a cabinet of odd categories: magazine, kiosk, books, activities, archive, submissions, support. It has an eccentric intellectual structure without feeling like a modern SaaS page.
- Borrow: cabinet/shelf navigation, many doors into one mind-space, small images as artifacts, taxonomic weirdness.
- Do not borrow: tiny unreadable nav, early-web clutter, donation/shop/account sprawl, or literal "cabinet of curiosities" styling.

### Candidate 2C — Indexhibit

- URL: https://www.indexhibit.org/
- Why: The artist-site convention of index on one side and exhibit on the other is a strong fit for "自分の考え画廊": the work is navigated as a personal inventory, not sold as a product.
- Borrow: index-first structure, left-side table-of-contents energy, one active exhibit area, intentionally plain navigation.
- Do not borrow: default-template plainness, anonymous black-and-white sameness, or making the final page feel unfinished.

## Candidate Set 3 — Cabinet + More Museum-Like Options

The user asked to keep Cabinet and replace the other directions with references
that feel more museum-like.

### Candidate 3A — Cabinet Magazine

- URL: https://www.cabinetmagazine.org/
- Why: It is not a museum, but it has the strongest "private intellectual cabinet" structure: magazine, kiosk, archive, books, activities, submissions, and many small doors into a mind-space.
- Borrow: categorized entrances, taxonomic weirdness, small artifact image energy, an archive that feels curated by a person.
- Do not borrow: tiny unreadable early-web clutter, donation/shop/account sprawl, or literal cabinet-of-curiosity styling.

### Candidate 3B — Letterform Archive

- URL: https://letterformarchive.org/
- Why: A real archive/museum hybrid. It has collection, exhibition, publishing, education, and online archive layers, which maps well to CreativeVault as a homepage for works, notes, and experiments.
- Borrow: archive-as-institution feeling, exhibit/publishing split, object-image + text rhythm, typography-led museum authority.
- Do not borrow: nonprofit/member/donate clutter, generic event/news density, or type-history specificity.

### Candidate 3C — The Menil Collection

- URL: https://www.menil.org/
- Why: More clearly museum/campus-like than Dia for this purpose: collection, research, archives, library, on-view works, events, and reflective atmosphere. It suggests a personal "neighborhood of rooms" rather than a grid.
- Borrow: campus/rooms metaphor, reflective museum copy, collection + research + archive navigation, image-led exhibition sections.
- Do not borrow: institutional welcome-copy blandness, equal card grids, or free-admission visitor logistics.
