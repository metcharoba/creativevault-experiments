## Decomposed from the screenshot
- **Composition:** A narrow announcement strip sits at the very top. Under it is a white navigation/header bar with menu on the left, a centered red identity mark, and utility icons/buttons on the right. The hero image is full-width and tall, cropped hard so only parts of very large letterforms and color fields are visible. A black label panel overlays the lower-left of the hero image. Below the hero image, a white thesis block uses an oversized red headline, a small black subline/CTA, and a large red circular mark on the right. The next section begins immediately with a big red section heading and three dense content entries.
- **Whitespace / ma:** Whitespace is not austere; it works as a gallery wall around heavy red/black typography. The page alternates dense visual fields with open white breathing room. The hero label is tight and grounded, while the thesis block has a lot of left and right air.
- **Material / texture:** The visual material comes from printed typography and reproduction texture: yellow paper, black shapes, red and blue letterforms, scan grain. For CreativeVault, the hero material should be a self-made typographic specimen/collage rather than borrowed art.
- **Typography (pairing, contrast, scale):** Big bold rounded sans-serif dominates. Red is used for identity and section emphasis. Supporting text is black, compact, and heavy. The visual image itself is also typography. There is little typographic delicacy; the confidence comes from scale and bluntness.
- **Information density:** First-view is rich but legible: announcement, nav, hero image, label, thesis, and the start of a three-column section. It feels institutional but active, not minimal.
- **Non-uniformity (how same-kind elements differ):** The first content row has three entries with similar layout rules but different image color, title length, and category/date rhythm. The hero image differs from cards: it is not an image card but a cropped visual field with an overlay label.

## Borrow vs don't-borrow
- **Borrow:** Top announcement strip; white nav bar; centered red identity; full-width cropped typographic hero image; black lower-left exhibition label; oversized red thesis; start of a three-column "on view" row visible below the hero checkpoint.
- **Don't-borrow:** Do not copy Letterform's logo, seal, exact red mark, exact artwork, exact text, membership/donation utilities, or event/news content. Do not use real collection images from the reference.

## Avoid-list (house-tics banned this build)
- No halftone.
- No print misregistration.
- No cream/bone paper background.
- No condensed poster type or comic bursts.
- No SIGNAL/transmission/fragments/future vocabulary.
- No SaaS cards, pill CTAs, frosted panels, or centered marketing hero.
- Red is allowed here because the chosen lead uses red as an institutional identity color; avoid making it the old cream/red/blue house palette by keeping the base white and using self-made typography.
