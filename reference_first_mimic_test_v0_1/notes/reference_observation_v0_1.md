# Reference Observation — Reference-first mimic test v0.1

Created: 2026-06-12
Scope: CreativeVault / design system evaluation (NOT CreativeWorld / NMG).
Method note: references decomposed at a **structural/composition level** from their
well-known design language. No source code, copy, imagery, fonts, colors, or layout
was copied. No reference screenshots were saved into this project.

## 1. Candidates (real, strong, non-SaaS)

### A. Klim Type Foundry — klim.co.nz  ★ chosen lead
- **Why good:** Among the strongest editorial type-specimen sites. The page is built
  *around oversized typography as the main event*, not around UI cards. Rigorous
  baseline rhythm, restrained palette, long-form reading, dramatic scale jumps.
- **Borrow:** oversized typographic masthead as the hero visual; editorial baseline
  rhythm; asymmetric text measures; multiple dramatic type scales on one screen;
  a ruled "specimen / index" list instead of cards.
- **Don't borrow:** their actual typefaces, exact grid, any copy, the strict
  black-on-white palette, any specimen imagery.

### B. Risotto Studio — risottostudio.com  (material reference)
- **Why good:** Material-forward risograph studio. Identity = the *imperfect print
  surface*: limited spot inks, overprint, paper grain, visible registration offset.
  Reads as printed matter, not a web template.
- **Borrow:** riso 2-ink duotone overprint; paper grain; deliberate registration
  mis-alignment between colour layers as a texture.
- **Don't borrow:** exact colourways, product photos, logo, shop/commerce layout.

### C. Bureau Borsche — bureauborsche.com  (layout-attitude reference)
- **Why good:** Anti-polish editorial studio. Aggressive scale contrast (huge display
  vs tiny mono caption), intentional grid-breaking, magazine-spread energy.
- **Borrow:** huge-vs-tiny scale contrast; grid-breaking / overlap placement;
  editorial index treatment for a body of work.
- **Don't borrow:** project imagery, client names, navigation, colour.

### D. Pangram Pangram — pangrampangram.com  (extra)
- **Why good:** bold flat colour field behind oversized type, grain over flat colour,
  playful asymmetry.
- **Borrow:** flat single colour field + grain (instead of dark gradient + cards).
- **Don't borrow:** brand colours, fonts, mascot, copy.

## 2. Chosen lead + blend

Lead structural reference: **Klim Type Foundry** (oversized editorial typography +
ruled index, no cards). Surface material: **Risotto** (riso duotone + grain +
registration offset). Layout attitude: **Bureau Borsche** (scale break + overlap).

## 3. Decomposition of the lead (what generic AI LP lacks)

- **First view:** NOT a centred hero. A left-anchored oversized type lockup that
  overlaps a printed plate bleeding off the right edge. The type IS the image.
- **Whitespace:** asymmetric. Wide empty paper on one side, dense ruled ledger on
  the other. Not evenly distributed "clean" whitespace.
- **Typography:** condensed industrial display (caps) for the masthead, high-contrast
  wonky serif italic for the literary colophon, mono for meta — three voices, big
  scale contrast.
- **Background:** flat warm newsprint paper + grain. No dark gradient, no glow.
- **Image/material:** the only "image" is a self-made generative halftone plate
  rendered in two inks — printed-matter, not a photo or a floating card.
- **Motion:** near-still; the plate breathes slowly; blocks reveal once on load.
- **Information density:** an editorial ledger of plates (numbered rows), dense and
  ruled — opposite of a sparse card grid.
- **Grid-breaking:** masthead and plate overlap; meta + rubber stamps placed off-grid
  and rotated; ledger rows use uneven indents.
- **Where it differs from generic AI LP:** no eyebrow→H1→subtitle→pill stack, no
  frosted cards, no orb, no purple gradient — it reads as a *printed poster*, the
  imperfection (misregistration, grain) is kept on purpose.
